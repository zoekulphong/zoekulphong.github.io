Viridia

redesigning a plant care app

Viridia’s mobile app provides plant-care notifications personalized to plants in your very own virtual garden.

challenges

Redesigning an entire app is a highly involved process. Viridia had not previously invested in real design work. Their app lacked a visually pleasing user interface and had several problems that were identified throughout the app and addressed in this redesign.

This project is broken down into six sections, each with their own unique user flows, challenges, and solutions.

solutions

Login & Signup

The Login & Signup pages allow users to easily login and signup for the Viridia mobile app.

Problems with the previous app design were primarily aesthetic. In this redesign, the option to login and signup with Google was added to provide a simpler option.

Home

The Home page is the first page that users see when they open the Viridia mobile app. Here, users see the upcoming tasks for all of their plants. Each task can be opened for more information and can also be marked as complete.

The main problems with the Home page’s previous design were aesthetic. In this redesign, tasks open as pop-ups, and can be marked as complete from this pop-up. This creates more continuity within the app. The option to edit the task’s description and frequency directly from the home page was added to simplify the number of steps in this user flow. Plant birthdays were added to allow users to easily track the growth of their plants.

All of your plants' tasks, all in one place

Explore

The Explore page allows users to view Viridia’s entire catalog of plants and search for new plants.

There were several problems with the previous design’s Explore page. Not all plants were directly accessible via the Explore page. Additionally, there was no way to sort plants, and they did not have adequate information. Adding a plant was overwhelming, and the call to action to add a plant to the user’s garden was buried within the page.

In this redesign, all plants are showcased directly on the Explore page and can be sorted by collection. More information for each plant was added. Adding a plant now opens as a pop-up and is broken down into easy-to-digest steps.

1. Name your plant and snap a profile picture

2. Customize your plant’s care or use Viridia’s recommended settings

3. Set your notifications and never miss a task!

My Garden

User’s plants are housed, and can be edited and removed, in My Garden. Tasks can be marked as complete and opened to view more information, and users can find plants similar to those already in their gardens.

In the previous My Garden page, plants lacked adequate information, and the process of editing a plant was overwhelming. It was also not possible to edit the notification time of an individual plant.

In this redesign, more information was added for each plant. The process of editing a plant was broken down into multiple sections and now presents as a pop-up for continuity. It is now easy to edit the notification time of an individual plant, and users can easily find plants similar to those in their gardens.

Watch your garden grow and discover more plants to love

Favorites

Plants that users save appear in the Favorites page. Here, users can view their favorite plants and add them to their gardens.

A minor problem with the previous design was the icon used to represent the Favorites page. A star was previously used, but did not fit the design of the app. Additionally, the process of adding a plant to the user’s garden was not clear.

In the redesign, the star was changed to a heart icon. As when adding plants from other pages, a clear call to action to add a plant to the user’s garden was included and this process was broken down into multiple sections and now opens as a pop-up for simplicity.

Profile

The Profile page is where users go to logout of the app and edit their profile.

The major problem with the previous design was that users could not logout of the app. Additionally, the previous design lacked an aesthetically-pleasing interface.

In the redesign, a logout button was clearly added and the option to edit notification times was moved to individual plants in the users’ garden. Additionally, the option to edit the user’s profile was made more clear.

style

The Viridia mobile app’s simple color palette was chosen to reflect colors commonly found in nature and create a peaceful yet lively aesthetic. It consists of a deep viridian and a lighter viridian, an energizing orange and a paler orange, a leafy green, an elegant neutral, and a dark text color.

conclusion

The Viridia mobile app is currently available on the Apple Store and Google Play Store and this redesign is still in the process of being implemented. I had never tackled such an intensive project before, and this redesign taught me a lot about the importance of simplifying and streamlining design processes.
